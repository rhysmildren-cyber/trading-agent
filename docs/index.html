WHAT AM I LOOKING AT? — the experiment, in plain English

The setup. Four robot monsters each trade a pretend $100,000 with one simple, locked-in rule. Once a day at 10:05am Brisbane time they check the Bitcoin price, make one decision, and sleep. Most days: nothing. They all race the lazy rival — a baseline that bought Bitcoin on day one and never touches it. Beating it after fees is the whole game.

Each monster's page (click a card) walks through today's decision with the actual numbers, shows the exact price that would make it buy or sell next, and tracks it against the expectation we wrote down before launch.

The fine print. Rules were locked before launch and never get tuned mid-race. Every trade pays realistic fees (0.35%). Safety rails on everyone: lose >10% in a day → 24-hour bench; fall 25% below start → shut down until a human looks. With four racers the leader is partly luck, so judgement happens at day 90, against the baseline and the pre-registered expectations — not the daily leaderboard. No real money anywhere.
