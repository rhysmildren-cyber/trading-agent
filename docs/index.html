WHAT AM I LOOKING AT? — the swarm, in plain English

The experiment. Four robot monsters each trade a pretend $100,000 using one simple, locked-in rule. They check the Bitcoin price once a day at 10:05am Brisbane time, make one decision, and sleep. Most days: nothing. Expect a trade or two a month each. All of them race the lazy rival — a baseline that bought Bitcoin on day one and never touches it.

KEPLER — Long above the 50-day average (±1% buffer), cash below. Betting that trends persist.

VECTOR — Long while the last 30 days are net positive, cash when negative. Betting that winners keep winning.

DONNIE — Buy a 20-day-high close, exit on a 10-day low. Betting that new highs beget new highs.

DIP — Buy panic (RSI below 30), sell relief (RSI above 60). Betting that panic overshoots — the anti-trend control.

HOUSTON — the grey one — never trades. It keeps score, flags broken rules, and reminds us of the fine print below.

The fine print. With four racers, whoever's on top is partly just lucky — like the luckiest of four coin-flippers. So nobody gets judged on the leaderboard alone: each monster passes or fails against the baseline and its own backtest expectation after 90 days. Rules were locked before launch and never tuned mid-race. Every trade pays realistic fees (0.35%). Safety rails on everyone: lose >10% in a day → 24h bench; fall 25% below start → shut down until a human looks. No real money anywhere.
